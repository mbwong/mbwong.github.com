About Me

I am a Research Professional at the Booth School of Business at the University of Chicago. I am passionate about using data creatively to illuminate patterns in human behavior. On a typical day I write R and Python code, apply econometric analysis, and read papers.

I have dabbled in management consulting, entrepreneurship, and product development. Previously I worked as a business consultant at APT, a co-founder of an event aggregation start-up, and physics researcher in the Hoffman Lab.

I grew up in Hong Kong. I drink tea, not coffee. I bike to work and love Honey-nut Cheerios. I like to make friends smile when they are upset. Thinkers who influenced me include Danny Kahneman, Paul Graham, Thich Nhat Hanh, Marcus Aurelius, and Reinhold Niebuhr.

This is what I look like, in case you were curious.
