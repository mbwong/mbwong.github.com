Briefly

I live in San Francisco and work for Applied Predictive Technologies (APT). I recently graduated from Harvard, earning a bachelors in physics with highest honors.

I write here on a broad range of topics. I also catalog some of the code I've written here.

More

I grew up in Hong Kong, attended high school in New Hampshire, and college in Boston. I can speak Cantonese and Mandarin fluently.

I like a lot of things, but especially analyzing large datasets, building software, thinking about product design and distribution, economics, and human computation.

Prior to APT, I crunched data for Freakonomics author Steve Levitt and also at McKinsey & Co. In college, I worked as a research assistant at the Harvard Physics Department and a teaching fellow for Harvard’s CS51.

I often program in my free time. I created an online campus events calendar Eventplease and am part of the Place Pulse team at the MIT Media Lab. You can view some of the code I've written here.

I like food from all corners of the world. I enjoy pinot noir, puer tea, and decaffienated coffee. Friends generally classify my musical taste as “indie”, but I enjoy classical, jazz, and occasionally rap as well.

My politics are best described as pragmatic. Broadly speaking, I am a social liberal and a fiscal moderate. If I am unsure about a topic, I generally defer to The Economist newspaper.
